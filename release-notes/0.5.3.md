## Added

- **Double edge** (move #22), mined from the actual Hennessy & Patterson chapter-2 exercise set: a single design change argued through *both* of its mechanisms — how it helps AND how the very same change hurts, or the condition deciding which edge dominates (H&P 2.16.3: "how could this change decrease program size? How could it increase it?"). Generalizes to caching, indexing, dosage, policy — any one-change-two-mechanisms setup.

## Changed

- **Counterexample hunt got stricter**: where the material supports it, a broken universal claim now demands the minimal breaking case *or the exact range of inputs where it fails* (H&P 2.11 style: "what range of values causes overflow?"), never a hand-wave that it "sometimes" fails.
- Validation note: mapping the full exercise set confirmed 0.5.2's three moves are the backbone of problem-book style — over half of H&P chapter 2's exercises are faithful-translation or resource-minimality instances, and "what do we know for certain" (2.33) is necessary-vs-possible verbatim.
