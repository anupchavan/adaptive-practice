Live-feedback release: the first real sessions on 0.5.0 (Anthropic + Gemini) showed exactly where the analytical moves lost their teeth. All fixes are domain-general.

## Changed

- **Traps are experienced, not narrated.** The moves kept collapsing into one template: "A learner argues X. Why is this wrong?" — the stem discloses the verdict, so the learner articulates someone else's mistake instead of risking their own. New rules: a stem may present a claim, derivation, or approach but never reveals whether it is correct; "why is this wrong" phrasing is banned; the "someone argues" frame is capped at one per session. The deep-authoring attack pass now hunts verdict leaks too.
- **A hard question must leave two options standing.** Distractors were one plausible answer plus three eliminable-by-tone. Hard MCQs now require at least two options that are conclusions of complete, plausible reasoning chains diverging at exactly one step — the classic form being the same claim with the direction, boundary, or quantifier flipped. If elimination works without touching the mechanism, the question isn't hard.
- **Re-skins count as repeats.** One session asked the same network-vs-Internet question three times wearing different city names. Continuation and top-up prompts now define a repeat as the same point in a new costume; a second question on a covered concept must probe a different aspect through a different move.
- **The moves are enforced from the user prompt as well.** One provider ignored the system-prompt catalog entirely and produced twenty definitional questions. Every medium and hard question is now explicitly required to instantiate a move; "what is X" phrasing is reserved for easy questions on genuinely factual material.
- **Explanations open by naming the trap** — when a question has a tempting wrong path, the explanation's first sentence says what it was and why it fails, so a wrong answer teaches the pattern, not just the fact.

## Fixed

- **Source-note links in stems gave the answer's category away.** Two culprits: a leftover 0.4.3 calibration step still injected `[[wikilinks]]` to source notes into stems whenever the stem mentioned the topic title (removed — 0.4.4 had reversed this decision everywhere else), and model-written links that resolve to real notes slipped past the render guard, which only demoted dead links. All internal links in question content are now unwrapped to plain text at parse time; image embeds, external URLs, and code spans (bash `[[ -f x ]]` stays intact) are untouched. The source note still appears after you answer.
