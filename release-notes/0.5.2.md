## Added

- **Three new analytical moves**, mined from the reasoning patterns of classic architecture problem sets (Hennessy & Patterson chapter exercises) and, as always, domain-general:
  - **Faithful translation** — the same content in two representations (formula↔code, spec↔implementation, source↔target language): produce the faithful counterpart, or spot the one subtle infidelity among near-miss translations.
  - **Resource minimality** — what is the *minimum* of a bounded resource (steps, memory, queries, assumptions) that still works; distractors are correct-but-wasteful or infeasibly tight.
  - **Capacity limit** — a representation has a fixed budget (bits, range, precision, slots): what exactly fits, what happens one step past the limit, and which workaround the system needs beyond it.
- **Move weighting by material kind.** Procedural/formal notes lean on translation, minimality, capacity, boundaries, and flawed arguments; conceptual notes lean on false symmetry, minimal pairs, and causal direction; factual notes on necessary-vs-possible and comparisons. The model now picks moves with the note's epistemic family in mind. Catalog: 21 moves.
